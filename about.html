ABOUT HOME GALLERY


The history of photography has always been moving towards making the photograph itself invisible and leave image as the only information. However my photography strives to renounce the photograph as an objective and perfect recreation of a scene. I am instead focused on the way that we perceive and interact with images, and how different photographic processes can interfere with and effect that interaction. I work with obsolete and damaged materials to create images which expose the physicality of the print and the photographic process.

To do this I have taken photos with an area of devoid of information that I placed in the scene, when printed it leaves only the traces of the photographic process. This breaks the illusion of the photograph being a window into a scene and exposes it as a physical object.

I photograph using destroyed and damaged film, this hinders the cameras ability to accurately reproduce the scene. I do this to interfere with the viewers perception of the content of my images and to force them to start seeing them as photographs.

I want my photos to make people consider how photos have come to be reflexively analysed for their content over their physicality. This is a problem I think afflicts photography and stops it from being fully realised as an art form.
